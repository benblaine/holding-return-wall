Methodology // how to read the wall

What the wall shows, and what it doesn't.

← Back to the wall

Rolling holding return

For a hold length of N years and a buy month t, the return is Price(t + N years) / Price(t), plotted at the buy month t. A value of 2× means money doubled; 0.5× means it halved. Each coloured line is one hold length; the curve traces how that N-year outcome changed depending on when you bought. Because you cannot realise an N-year hold that hasn't finished yet, every line ends N years before the last data month — a young asset simply has no long-horizon lines. We never fabricate, pad, or extrapolate missing history.

Total return, in USD

Equities use dividend- and split-adjusted close, so the lines reflect total return, not just price. Everything is denominated in US dollars. Currency tiles show the value of one unit of that currency in USD. Crypto and macro series are mixed in at monthly resolution, which is plenty for multi-year holds and keeps daily-vs-weekly trading calendars from fighting.

Base assets only — no derivatives

Tiles are the underlying asset itself. Metals are spot (gold, silver, platinum, palladium), never the futures contracts and never the metal ETFs. Index funds are not tiles: the S&P 500 total return and US inflation (CPI) appear only as optional benchmark overlays — a thin reference line on each tile plus a beat S&P / beat inflation badge at the active hold length.

Survivorship — read with care

The universe is a deliberately mixed, curated set: roughly a third generational winners, a third famous disasters and boom-busts, a third steady tortoises, chosen so the wall teaches contrast rather than “everything goes up.” Even so, it is a list of assets that exist today. Companies that went to zero and delisted are largely absent, so the wall still understates how badly individual bets can end. A tile that’s awaiting its price feed renders as a labelled placeholder, never a guess.

Not a forecast

These are past returns — history, not predictions. Nothing here implies what any asset will do next. Long, calm-looking green stretches were not knowable in advance, and the same buy date can look brilliant or ruinous depending only on the hold length you pick. Use the wall to build intuition about ranges and dispersion, not to pick winners.
